# Inter-Market Analysis & Macro Correlations

## Why Inter-Market Analysis Matters
No market moves in isolation. Every asset is influenced by other markets. Understanding these relationships gives you an "unfair advantage" — you can anticipate moves in one market by watching another.

---

## THE BIG FOUR: STOCKS, BONDS, COMMODITIES, CURRENCIES

### 1. Stocks & Bonds (Usually Inverse)
- **Bonds UP, Stocks DOWN**: Risk-off environment, investors flee to safety
- **Bonds DOWN (yields UP), Stocks UP**: Risk-on environment, investors prefer returns
- **Yield Curve**: When short-term yields > long-term yields = inverted curve = recession warning
- **When both fall together**: Severe risk-off (rare but very dangerous — 2022 style)

### 2. USD & Commodities (Usually Inverse)
- **DXY UP → Commodities DOWN** (Gold, Oil, Copper all priced in USD — stronger USD = lower prices)
- **DXY DOWN → Commodities UP**
- **Exception**: Sometimes both rise simultaneously in inflationary environments

### 3. Gold & USD (Usually Inverse)
- Gold is the anti-dollar hedge
- DXY rising = Gold under pressure
- DXY falling = Gold supported
- **Exception**: Geopolitical fear can push gold up even with strong USD

### 4. Risk-On vs Risk-Off Framework

**Risk-ON (investors are confident):**
- Stocks ↑, High-yield assets ↑
- USD ↓ (vs commodity currencies), JPY ↓, CHF ↓
- Gold flat/down, Bonds flat/down (yields up)
- Crypto ↑ (generally moves with risk appetite)
- AUD ↑, NZD ↑, CAD ↑ (commodity currencies benefit)

**Risk-OFF (investors are fearful):**
- Stocks ↓, High-yield assets ↓
- USD ↑, JPY ↑ (safe havens), CHF ↑
- Gold ↑, Bonds ↑ (yields down), Treasuries ↑
- Crypto ↓ (risk asset behavior)
- AUD ↓, NZD ↓, CAD ↓

## DXY (US Dollar Index) — The Master Indicator

The DXY measures USD strength against a basket of major currencies (EUR 57.6%, JPY 13.6%, GBP 11.9%, CAD 9.1%, SEK 4.2%, CHF 3.6%).

### DXY Impact on Forex Pairs
| DXY Direction | EUR/USD | GBP/USD | USD/JPY | Gold | Crypto |
|---------------|---------|---------|---------|------|--------|
| DXY ↑ Strong | ↓ Falls | ↓ Falls | ↑ Rises | ↓ Falls | ↓ Usually |
| DXY ↓ Weak | ↑ Rises | ↑ Rises | ↓ Falls | ↑ Rises | ↑ Usually |

### Key DXY Levels to Watch
- **DXY above 105**: Strong dollar — pressure on EUR/USD, Gold, BTC
- **DXY below 100**: Weak dollar — supportive for commodities, alts, EUR/GBP
- **DXY at 200-day EMA**: Key decision point — break determines near-term trend

### How to Use DXY for Crypto
- Inverse correlation: BTC and DXY often move in opposite directions
- When DXY breaks key resistance = likely BTC headwind
- When DXY breaks key support = potential BTC tailwind
- Correlation is not 100% — check with current macro environment

## US TREASURY YIELDS (US10Y)

The US 10-year treasury yield is the most important interest rate in the world.

### Yield Movements & Market Impact
- **Yields RISING**: Federal Reserve hawkish, or economic growth strong
  - USD strengthens
  - Growth stocks (tech, crypto) often fall
  - Gold under pressure
  - Emerging market currencies fall

- **Yields FALLING**: Fed dovish, or recession fears/risk-off
  - USD weakens
  - Growth assets rally
  - Gold supported
  - Risk assets benefit

### Yield Levels
- **Below 3%**: Supportive for risk assets, crypto tends to perform well
- **3–4%**: Neutral zone, mixed impact
- **Above 4.5%**: Pressure on risk assets, crypto headwind, mortgage/credit stress
- **Above 5%**: Significant risk-off pressure historically

## SPX (S&P 500) & NASDAQ CORRELATION TO CRYPTO

### Historical Correlation Periods
- 2020–2022: Very high correlation (BTC and SPX moved nearly in lockstep)
- When correlation is high: A stock market crash = crypto crash
- When correlation breaks down (crypto moves independently): Usually driven by crypto-specific catalysts (halving, ETF approvals, major hacks)

### How to Use SPX for Crypto Trading
- If SPX is in a clear downtrend: Avoid new crypto long positions
- If SPX breaks key support: Expect crypto to follow within hours/days
- SPX earnings seasons (quarterly): Can create volatility spikes that bleed into crypto
- SPX VIX (Volatility Index):
  - VIX < 15: Complacency, risk-on — good for crypto
  - VIX 15–25: Normal caution
  - VIX > 30: Fear, risk-off — bad for crypto
  - VIX > 40: Panic, potentially capitulation bottom forming

## GOLD (XAU/USD) ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK

Gold is the ultimate safe-haven and inflation hedge. It has 5,000 years of history as money.

### What Drives Gold
1. **Real Interest Rates** (most important): Real Rate = Nominal Rate – Inflation
   - Real rates negative = Gold rallies (holding gold costs less relative to bonds)
   - Real rates positive = Gold under pressure (bonds pay more than inflation)
   
2. **USD strength**: DXY up = Gold down (inverse relationship)

3. **Geopolitical risk**: Wars, sanctions, instability = Gold up (safe haven buying)

4. **Central bank buying**: CBs globally have been buying gold record amounts — structural support

5. **Inflation expectations**: CPI rising = Gold benefits as inflation hedge

### Gold Price Levels (Key psychological and technical)
- $2,000: Major psychological support (now below = bearish, above = bullish)
- $2,500: Next major resistance/support
- $3,000+: Extreme bull scenario, only in stagflation/crisis

### Gold vs Crypto
- Both can act as "inflation hedges" but in different environments
- Institutional money prefers Gold for safety
- Retail and tech-forward investors prefer BTC ("digital gold")
- When Gold makes new ATH = monetary system stress = watch BTC for follow-through rally

## OIL (WTI/BRENT) CORRELATIONS

### Oil Impact on Currencies
- **Oil rises → CAD strengthens** (Canada major oil exporter)
- **Oil rises → USD/CAD falls** (bullish CAD)
- **Oil rises → JPY weakens** (Japan major oil importer)
- **Oil rises → Inflation pressures up → Fed hawkish** → USD can strengthen

### Oil Impact on Markets
- Oil spike = inflationary pressure = central banks tighten = risk assets fall
- Oil crash = deflationary pressure = stimulus likely = risk assets can rally
- Watch: OPEC meetings (production cuts/increases), US inventory data (weekly)

## PRACTICAL INTER-MARKET CHECKLIST

Before any major trade, answer these questions:

**For Crypto Longs:**
- [ ] Is DXY in a downtrend or at resistance? (favorable if yes)
- [ ] Are US10Y yields falling or stable? (favorable if yes)
- [ ] Is SPX in an uptrend? (favorable if yes)
- [ ] Is VIX below 20? (favorable if yes)
- [ ] Is Gold stable/rising (not in panic)? (neutral/favorable)
- Score 4–5: Green light for crypto longs with confidence
- Score 2–3: Proceed with caution, smaller positions
- Score 0–1: Avoid new longs, risk-off environment

**For Forex USD Shorts (EUR/USD long, etc.):**
- [ ] Is DXY showing bearish pattern or at resistance?
- [ ] Are Fed rate cut expectations rising? (CME FedWatch)
- [ ] Is US economic data disappointing (weak NFP, CPI cooling)?
- [ ] Is risk-on sentiment present (SPX rising, VIX falling)?
- Score 3–4: Strong setup for USD short plays

## MACRO CALENDAR — MOST IMPORTANT DATES

### Monthly High-Impact (All times affect all markets)
- **First Friday**: US Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) — biggest monthly event
- **Second/Third Week**: US CPI — inflation data, critical for Fed
- **Late Month**: PCE inflation (Fed's preferred measure)
- **Monthly**: FOMC Minutes, FOMC Meetings (8/year)

### How to Read Macro Surprises
- What matters is NOT the number itself — it's **actual vs forecast**
- Beat expectations significantly = hawkish surprise = USD up, risk assets down
- Miss expectations = dovish surprise = USD down, risk assets up
- "Buy the rumor, sell the news" — often price already moved in anticipation
