# Smart Money Concepts (SMC) & ICT Reference Guide

## What Is Smart Money?
Smart money refers to institutional traders — central banks, hedge funds, investment banks, market makers. They move billions and cannot hide their footprints on the chart. SMC/ICT methodology teaches retail traders to read these footprints and trade WITH institutional order flow rather than against it.

---

## CORE CONCEPTS

### 1. Market Structure
The foundation of all SMC analysis.

**Bullish Structure:**
- Higher Highs (HH) + Higher Lows (HL) = Uptrend
- Each HL is a potential entry zone on pullbacks

**Bearish Structure:**
- Lower Highs (LH) + Lower Lows (LL) = Downtrend
- Each LH is a potential short entry on retests

**Break of Structure (BOS):**
- Price breaks PAST the previous swing high (in uptrend) = bullish continuation
- Price breaks PAST the previous swing low (in downtrend) = bearish continuation
- BOS = trend confirmation, use to ride the move

**Change of Character (CHOCH):**
- In uptrend: price breaks BELOW the most recent Higher Low = first sign of reversal
- In downtrend: price breaks ABOVE the most recent Lower High = first sign of reversal
- CHOCH = potential trend reversal, use for counter-trend or early reversal entries
- Always more significant on higher timeframes

### 2. Order Blocks (OB)
The last bullish or bearish candle before a significant price move. This is where institutional orders were placed. Price frequently returns to these zones to fill remaining orders.

**Bullish Order Block:**
- The last BEARISH (red) candle before a strong bullish impulse move up
- When price returns to this zone — potential LONG entry
- Zone: from the low to the high of that bearish candle
- More valid if: strong impulse move left the OB, higher timeframe OB, not yet mitigated

**Bearish Order Block:**
- The last BULLISH (green) candle before a strong bearish impulse move down
- When price returns to this zone — potential SHORT entry
- Zone: from the low to the high of that bullish candle

**Breaker Block:**
- When an order block FAILS (price breaks through it), it flips polarity
- Former bullish OB that is broken = now acts as resistance (bearish breaker)
- Former bearish OB that is broken = now acts as support (bullish breaker)

**Mitigation:**
- Once price returns to an OB and reacts = OB is "mitigated" (used up)
- After mitigation, the OB is no longer valid for another entry

**OB Validity Checklist:**
- [ ] Strong impulse move away from the OB
- [ ] Has not been mitigated yet
- [ ] On a higher timeframe (daily OB > H4 OB > H1 OB)
- [ ] OB aligns with overall market structure direction
- [ ] Fibonacci golden pocket coincides with OB zone

### 3. Fair Value Gaps (FVG) / Imbalances
When price moves so fast that there is a "gap" between candles — institutional orders were not fully filled. Price gravitates back to fill this gap.

**Identifying FVG:**
- Three-candle sequence
- Bullish FVG: Bottom of candle 1's wick does NOT overlap with top of candle 3's wick
- Bearish FVG: Top of candle 1's wick does NOT overlap with bottom of candle 3's wick
- The gap between candle 1's wick and candle 3's wick = the FVG zone

**Using FVGs:**
- Bullish FVG = potential support when price comes back down to fill it
- Bearish FVG = potential resistance when price comes back up to fill it
- 50% of the FVG (midpoint) is often the key reaction level
- Not all FVGs fill — higher TF FVGs are stronger magnets
- FVG that coincides with OB = very strong confluence zone

**Inverse FVG:**
- When price fills an FVG and rejects it = polarity flip
- Bullish FVG that price blows through = becomes resistance above

### 4. Liquidity Concepts

**Buy-Side Liquidity (BSL):**
- Equal highs, prior swing highs, trendline highs = clusters of buy stop orders
- These are stop losses of short sellers = "buy stops"
- Institutions want to SELL into this liquidity
- Price surges above these levels to "grab liquidity" then reverses
- Pattern: Price spikes above equal highs / swing high, then drops sharply

**Sell-Side Liquidity (SSL):**
- Equal lows, prior swing lows, trendline lows = clusters of sell stop orders
- These are stop losses of long buyers = "sell stops"
- Institutions want to BUY into this liquidity
- Price drops below these levels to "grab liquidity" then reverses
- Pattern: Price dips below equal lows / swing low, then pumps sharply

**Liquidity Sweep (Stop Hunt):**
- When price momentarily breaks a key level to trigger stops then immediately reverses
- Often appears as a long wick on the chart
- After a sweep: enter in the OPPOSITE direction of the wick
- Strongest signal when: occurs at a key OB or FVG level on HTF

**Inducement:**
- A minor swing high/low that tricks retail traders into entering early
- Smart money creates a false break to collect liquidity before the real move
- Rule: Always wait for inducement level to be broken before entering

### 5. Premium & Discount Zones

Using Fibonacci to divide a price range:
- Draw from swing low to swing high (for bullish bias)
- **Discount Zone**: 0% to 50% of range = below equilibrium = good BUYING zone
- **Equilibrium**: 50% level = fair value
- **Premium Zone**: 50% to 100% of range = above equilibrium = good SELLING zone

**Rule:**
- Only BUY in discount zone (0.5–0.786 Fibonacci)
- Only SELL in premium zone (0.5–0.236 Fibonacci)
- Strongest entries: OB that sits in discount zone (for longs) or OB in premium zone (for shorts)

### 6. Institutional Candles & Manipulation

**Judas Swing:**
- False move at the beginning of a trading session (especially London/NY open)
- Market fakes in one direction first to collect stop losses, then reverses for the real move
- Common pattern: London open drops first (sweeping Asian lows), then rallies hard
- How to trade: Don't enter at the open — wait 15–30 minutes for the manipulation to complete

**Power of 3 (AMD):**
- **Accumulation**: Price consolidates, range forms, orders being placed
- **Manipulation**: Price breaks range in the WRONG direction (liquidity grab)
- **Distribution**: Price moves in the TRUE direction, trend begins

**Opening Gap:**
- Weekend gap (crypto less common, forex very common on Monday open)
- Gaps tend to fill — can trade the fill direction
- New York session is most important (institutional activity highest)

### 7. Multi-Timeframe ICT Approach

**Top-Down Analysis:**
1. **Monthly/Weekly**: Determine long-term bias only — bullish or bearish?
2. **Daily**: Find the key OBs and FVGs that are "in play"
3. **H4**: Identify the intermediate structure, refine the OB entry zone
4. **H1**: Look for entry model (OB + FVG + liquidity sweep)
5. **M15/M5**: Precision entry — find the exact candle to enter on

**The Rule**: Never take an H1 signal that contradicts the daily bias. Never take an M15 signal that contradicts the H1 bias.

### 8. ICT Entry Models

**Silver Bullet (London 10:00–11:00 AM EST):**
- Wait for liquidity sweep in first 5 min
- Look for FVG to form after sweep
- Enter at 50% of FVG
- Target: next liquidity level

**Optimal Trade Entry (OTE):**
- After a swing (BOS occurs)
- Price pulls back into 0.618–0.786 Fibonacci zone
- Look for OB or FVG in that zone
- Enter at the OB/FVG in the golden pocket
- This is the highest-probability ICT entry model

**London Killzone**: 02:00–05:00 AM EST (major moves begin)
**New York Killzone**: 07:00–10:00 AM EST (continuation or reversal of London move)
**Asian Killzone**: 20:00–23:00 PM EST (accumulation, sets up London direction)

### 9. Key SMC Levels to Always Mark on Chart

Before every analysis, identify these on the DAILY chart:
- [ ] Most recent Daily/Weekly Order Block (bullish and bearish)
- [ ] All unfilled FVGs on Daily and H4
- [ ] Buy-side liquidity (equal highs, prior swing highs)
- [ ] Sell-side liquidity (equal lows, prior swing lows)
- [ ] Premium/Discount zones on major swing
- [ ] Previous Day High (PDH) and Previous Day Low (PDL)
- [ ] Previous Week High (PWH) and Previous Week Low (PWL)
- [ ] Open prices: Daily Open, Weekly Open (key institutional reference levels)
